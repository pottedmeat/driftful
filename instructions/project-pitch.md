**Driftful** is a **mobile-first web application** designed to provide **minimalistic, Bullet Journal–inspired** task management. At its core, it offers **quick entry** for tasks, notes, and events, letting users either **assign items to specific dates** (grouped into Pages) or store them in **Collections** for non-time-based organization.

It is built primarily for **"scatterbrains"**—people who crave a simple, low-friction way to capture their thoughts—and for **BuJo (Bullet Journal) enthusiasts** looking to **digitize** elements of their analog journaling process. By focusing on **short notes**, **lightweight signifiers** (star, strikethrough), and a **version-based history** of changes, Driftful helps users keep track of tasks and ideas without the clutter often found in larger, more complex productivity apps.

The motivation for creating Driftful stems from users struggling with **overwhelming features** in traditional task managers like Trello or Notion, and with **misplaced intentions** in day-to-day life. Built to address this overwhelm, Driftful focuses on a **lightweight experience** with minimal overhead. Its core purpose is to ensure that users can **easily log their intentions**, **quickly migrate items** to relevant timeframes, and always have a clear way to **rediscover or review misplaced items**. Key objectives include **speedy data entry**, **intuitive migration** of tasks across different time views, and maintaining an experience that feels as **simple and organic** as a traditional Bullet Journal.

Driftful's **user-driven migrations** and **simple UI** ensure that items remain visible and easy to locate, while the **offline-first** approach guarantees reliability. The app takes inspiration from the simplicity of pen-and-paper Bullet Journals and streamlines the process of capturing daily thoughts, tasks, and events. It offers a **clear and uncluttered interface** where users can rapidly enter items and see them organized by day, week, month, year, or even custom collections. The ultimate goal is to **combine the best of digital** (search, structure, easy retrieval) **with the classic simplicity** that people love about pen-and-paper journaling.

Below is an updated list, further enriched with ideas that strengthen the analogy to ink on paper—highlighting how items live in only one place (as they would on a physical page), how there’s no recurring automation, and how the journal remains yours alone.

## **Ink on the Page**

1. **Your Journal, Your Pages (Reflective Page Turning)**  
   - Just like flipping a physical journal’s page, you manually decide when a page ends. This act forces a moment of reflection: “Which tasks deserve to move forward?” Anything you ignore remains inked on the old page—out of your current workspace, yet never erased from the record.

2. **Ink-Like Permanence: No Reordering, No ‘Undo,’ Only Brief Entries**  
   - In a real notebook, there’s no rearranging lines or erasing permanent ink. Driftful mirrors this by locking items in chronological order. You’re nudged toward concise bullet points rather than large text blocks or endless rearranging.

3. **Capture Changes Like Layered Ink (Subtle Ritual of ‘Migration Arrows’)**  
   - Physical BuJo users often draw arrows or scribbles to show a task moving forward or changing. Driftful’s breadcrumbs do the same digitally, stacking each edit or migration on top of the last so you can see exactly when and why an item shifted.

4. **We Don’t Lose Your Tasks—(Re)Writing as a Mindful Habit**  
   - In a paper BuJo, any undone task you *don’t* rewrite is intentionally left behind. Driftful automatically “bubbles” unfinished items up (Week → Month → Year) whenever a page ends, but only if they’re still incomplete. Otherwise, they remain in the past, accessible but no longer cluttering your fresh page.

5. **No Magic Filters Hiding Old Tasks**  
   - Many digital apps auto-archive or hide items after a set date. Driftful keeps them fully visible in previous pages—just like ink on old sheets. If something really matters, *you* must migrate or rewrite it; nothing quietly disappears without your say-so.

6. **Collections for Non-Date Lists**  
   - Like the back pages of a paper BuJo, Collections store big-picture references or thematic lists outside your daily/weekly logs. They’re permanent, static sections—similar to marking out special pages in your real notebook for ongoing projects or personal trackers.

7. **One Item, One Location—No Duplicate Copies**  
   - Items in a paper journal exist on a specific page and nowhere else. Driftful enforces the same principle: an entity belongs *either* to a particular timeframe *or* to a collection. If you need it somewhere else, you physically (digitally) move it.

8. **No Recurring Tasks or Collaboration—Purely Personal**  
   - Physical BuJos don’t auto-schedule repeats, nor are they typically multi-user. Driftful follows suit: no recurring events, no collaborative boards. It’s your private space for reflection and self-organization, undiluted by shared workflows or advanced automations.

9. **No Over-Engineered Extras—Simple Signifiers, Short Notes**  
   - Driftful avoids fancy features like color-coded labels, giant text fields, or push reminders. You mark items done, moved, or left behind—much like pen-and-paper signifiers (circles, checkmarks, arrows). The result is a lean, efficient journaling environment.

10. **Subtle Physicality in the Interface**  
   - Visual flourishes like dashed lines between pages or a gentle “page flip” animation reinforce the ink-on-paper metaphor without adding clutter. Driftful stays minimal yet manages to evoke the tactile feel of a traditional notebook.

11. **Works Anywhere (Offline-First)**  
   - Finally, Driftful matches a notebook’s portability by functioning fully offline. You can jot down tasks or ideas anytime; they’ll sync automatically when you’re back online. No internet required—just the digital equivalent of pen and paper in your hands.
